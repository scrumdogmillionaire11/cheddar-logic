## 📊 Understanding Expected Value (EV)

If you only take one concept seriously in betting, make it this one.

**Expected Value (EV)** is how you decide whether a bet is **worth making**, not whether it will win.

---

## 🎯 What EV Actually Is

EV answers one question:

> “If I placed this same bet 1,000 times… would I make money?”

It’s a **long-run average**, not a prediction for tonight.

---

## 🧠 The Core Idea

Every bet has two ingredients:

* **Probability** (how often it should win)
* **Price** (what you’re getting paid)

EV is simply:

> Comparing those two things.

---

## 🔢 Simple Example

* You believe a bet wins **55%** of the time
* The book is pricing it like it wins **52.4%** (that’s -110)

That gap = **your edge**

Over time:

* You profit from that difference
* Even though you’ll still lose ~45% of the time

---

## ⚠️ What EV Is NOT

Let’s kill the biggest misconception:

> Positive EV ≠ “this will win”

You can:

* Make a great EV bet
* And lose

You can:

* Make a terrible EV bet
* And win

That’s variance doing its job.

---

## 🔑 Key Ideas

### 1. EV Is a Long-Run Game

You don’t “realize” EV in one bet.

You realize it over:

* 100 bets
* 500 bets
* 1,000+ bets

Short term = noise
Long term = signal

---

### 2. Always Compare EV (Don’t View It in Isolation)

A common mistake:

> “This looks like a good bet”

That’s useless thinking.

Instead:

* Is this better than other options on the slate?
* Is this the **best use of my bankroll right now?**

Sometimes the correct move is:

> “There are no good bets today.”

---

### 3. Small Edges Matter More Than Big Opinions

You don’t need:

* 70% win rates
* “Locks”

You need:

* Consistent **2–5% edges**

That’s where real money comes from.

---

### 4. EV Without Discipline Is Useless

You can find edge and still lose if you:

* Bet too much on one game
* Stack correlated bets
* Chase losses

That’s why EV must be paired with:

* **Risk control**
* **Exposure limits**

Exactly how the system enforces:

* No overexposure
* No forced plays
* Balanced card construction 

---

### 5. Not All EV Is Equal (Context Matters)

Two bets can both be +EV but very different:

* Low variance, steady edge
* High variance, volatile outcome

Smart bettors:

* Size differently
* Treat them differently

---

## 🧩 Practical EV Thinking

Instead of asking:

> “Do I like this bet?”

Ask:

* What probability am I assigning?
* What probability is the market implying?
* Is the gap meaningful?

If you can’t answer those:
👉 You’re guessing.

---

## 📉 Why Most Bettors Lose With EV

Because they:

* Overestimate their edge
* Ignore price
* Bet narratives instead of numbers
* Quit before EV has time to play out

Or worse:

* They only track wins/losses
* Instead of decision quality

---

## 🧀 The Cheddar Logic Angle

This system is built around EV discipline:

* No edge → PASS
* Thin edge → LEAN
* Clear edge → PLAY

Not because we’re cautious…

Because **forcing bad EV is how bankrolls die**.

---

## 📚 Educational Notes

* EV is about **process, not outcomes**
* Good bets lose all the time
* Bad bets win just enough to trick you

Your job isn’t to win today.

Your job is to:

> **Make decisions that would win over time.**

---

## ⚠️ Educational Disclaimer

This content is for educational purposes only.

* Expected value does not guarantee profit
* Short-term results can vary widely
* Even strong edges experience losing streaks

If you’re judging your bets by:

> “Did it win?”

You’re not using EV yet.

If you’re asking:

> “Was this priced wrong?”

Now you’re thinking correctly.
