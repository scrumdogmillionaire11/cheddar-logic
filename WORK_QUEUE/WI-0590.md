# WI-0590: Diagnose NBA totals underperformance before permanent retuning

**ID**: WI-0590
**Goal**: Produce a reproducible diagnostic report that determines whether NBA totals are failing because of projection bias, sigma width, or threshold aggression before any permanent model tuning is attempted.

**Scope**:
- `scripts/calibrate_nba_totals.js`
- `docs/runbooks/nba-totals-calibration-2026-03.md`

**Out of scope**:
- Shipping new NBA totals thresholds
- Shipping new NBA totals model coefficients
- Changing non-NBA markets

**Background**:
The right next step for NBA totals is diagnosis, not blind threshold tweaking. The market is materially negative in production, but that can come from different failure modes: projection bias, overly narrow/wide distribution assumptions, or an otherwise decent model being allowed to fire too often. This WI exists to answer that question with evidence instead of tuning by feel.

**Acceptance**:
- `scripts/calibrate_nba_totals.js` runs against settled production-style data and emits a report-ready analysis.
- The report covers at least the last 100 settled `nba-totals-call` rows, or all available settled rows if fewer than 100 exist.
- The report includes directional bias (`projected_total - actual_total`).
- The report includes MAE or RMSE of projected totals.
- The report includes over/under split performance.
- The report includes edge-bucket calibration (for example by 1-2%, 2-4%, 4%+ edge bands).
- The report includes a sigma sensitivity summary comparing current decision math assumptions against empirical history.
- The report ends with a forced recommendation: `model biased`, `sigma/plumbing issue`, `thresholds too loose`, or `insufficient sample`.
